What is a Credit Union?

Credit unions are not-for-profit financial institutions that offer similar products to commercial banks but operate very differently. Unlike traditional banks, credit unions are completely owned by their members and account-holders rather than shareholders, which means that they are often able to offer better products and better returns. Additionally, rather than serving the general public, credit unions only serve specific groups of members known as the field of membership. For LCUI, the field of membership includes all Columbia students, staff, faculty, alumni, and their immediate families.

The National Credit Union Administration (a federal agency) regulates credit unions, and accounts with federally-insured credit unions like LCUI are insured by the National Credit Union Share Insurance Fund up to $250,000 per individual.
